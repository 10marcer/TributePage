Allen Iverson

Pound for Pound the Greatest

Timeline of AI

  • June 1996: Iverson is drafted with the first overall pick by the 76ers
  • May 1997: Iverson is Rookie of the Year, averaging 23.5 points a game
  • January 1999: Iverson signs a six-year, $70 million contract extension with the 76ers.
  • May 1999: Iverson wins his first scoring title, averaging 26.8 points.
  • April 2001: Iverson wins his second scoring title, and is named league MVP. He also leads the Sixers to the NBA finals but they lose to the LA Lakers in 4 games
  • April 2002: Iverson wins his third scoring title, averaging 31.4 points a game
  • May 2002: After being eliminated in the first round, the infamous practice rant occurs
  • May 2003: Sixers coach Larry Brown, resigns from Sixers and coaches the Detroid Pistons
  • September 2003: Iverson agrees to a $76 million contract with Philadelphia
  • April 2004: Sixers miss the playoffs for the first time since 97-98
  • February 2005: Iverson scores a career-high 60 points in a win over the Orlando Magic
  • April 2006: Iverson averages 30.7 points and 7.9 assiste and wins his fourth scoring title
  • December 2006: Iverson is traded to the Denver Nuggets for Andre Miller, Joe Smith, and two 2007 first-round draft picks
